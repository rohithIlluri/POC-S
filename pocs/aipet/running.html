01

Vision

A creature lives in your terminal. It hatches from an egg, feeds on the tokens you already burn in Claude Code and Codex, and evolves based on how you work — not how much you pay. Good engineering hygiene raises a healthy, rare creature.

The existing collector/store engine already parses your session logs locally. That engine becomes the game's sensory organ. Everything cloud/dashboard-facing gets deleted.

PILLAR 01Zero backendEverything derives from local files. The only remote surface is GitHub — releases for distribution, gists/files for trading.
PILLAR 02Real play, no grindYou never play instead of working. Working is playing. The game reads logs; it never asks for artificial actions.
PILLAR 03Zero tokens, zero costThe game never calls a model. All simulation is deterministic local computation. $0 to run, $0 to operate.
PILLAR 04Craftsmanship is the metaThe old advisor rules — cache reuse, model routing, context hygiene — become care mechanics. Efficient devs raise stronger creatures.

⚖ IP note: "like Pokémon" means the genre. All names, species, art and lore are original. No Pokémon names, sprites, or trade dress will ever ship.

Lore — the Shellwoods

Beneath every filesystem lies the Shellwoods — a forest that grows in the warm exhaust of computation. When the first mainframes ran hot through the night, stray cycles condensed in the dark and became living things: Codelings, small creatures woven from discarded tokens and cache echoes.

Codelings feed on tokens — the crumbs of thought that fall when a developer converses with a machine. But they thrive on quality, not volume. A Codeling raised on cached, well-routed, tightly-scoped work grows bright-eyed and quick. One gorged on bloated contexts grows sluggish and dim — the Shellwoods call this token bloat.

Developers who bond with a Codeling are called Daemonkeepers — because the creature literally lives in a daemon.

Feed the mind, not the meter.— OLD DAEMONKEEPERS' PROVERB · THE GAME'S THESIS

The six regions of the Shellwoods

The Cachefen
A misty, silver-fog wetland where reflections echo back a half-beat early — the fen already knew you were about to speak. Boardwalks from earlier Keepers still hold over water so still it looks memoized.
"Third week here and I still haven't heard an original sound. 10/10, would memoize again." — K. Odera
The Contexta Canopy
Trunks thick as server racks, branches interleaving into a ceiling so dense the forest floor stays permanently dim. It holds everything ever grown into it, needed or not.
"Went up for one answer. Came down four hours later holding an entire project's history."
Runtime Ridge
A spine of dark volcanic rock, always mid-execution. Heat shimmer bends the horizon; vents hiss on cycles nobody's mapped. The least comfortable region — and the most satisfying.
"Set up camp for a focus sprint. Four hours passed like twenty minutes."
Syntax Thicket
A precise bramble maze — every branch meets every other at an angle that looks deliberate. Paths fork constantly; the Thicket punishes guessing and rewards reading the shape before committing.
"Asked a local for directions. Got a technically correct answer. Would ask again."
Streamfall
Waterfalls that scroll rather than fall — text-thin ribbons pouring down a canyon, catching light like a terminal that's never stopped outputting since it switched on.
"Sat by the falls for an hour just watching. Didn't catch anything. Best hour of the week."
The Daemon Deep
Below the roots of every other region — dark, low-ceilinged, never fully silent. A steady hum of background processes that never asked for an audience. Home to most rare and Relic sightings.
"Two hours in near-total dark, one very patient hum for company. Bring a headlamp, not a hurry."

The two Mythics — legends only

The First Process
Rumored to predate the Shellwoods' six regions entirely — not hatched, not condensed, just already running. Heard more often than seen: a hum just slightly too patient to be background noise, going on directly underneath everything else. Tied to the Daemon Deep and Runtime Ridge.
The Last Garbage Collector
Appears, so the legend goes, at the exact moment something would otherwise be lost for good — a context about to be dropped, a cache about to be evicted. Every account agrees on one thing: it never shows up the same way twice, and never to a Keeper who isn't about to lose something that mattered.

45 moves (36 type-pool + 9 starter signatures), 6 statuses, and a fully-specified integer battle algorithm. Two Keepers export battle cards; either machine replays the identical fight from a shared seed — no connection required, ever.

The core loop

              │
              ▼  deterministic sim tick

              │
              ▼

Feeding & health — the advisor, reborn

Tokens are food. Every old advisor rule becomes nutrition. Advice stays explainable — the pet's journal says why it feels how it feels.

LOW CACHE REUSE
Junk food. Uncached re-sent prompts digest poorly → health drips down, pet looks tired.
OPUS OVERUSE
Rich food. Fine sometimes; a steady diet makes the pet lethargic (XP multiplier drops).
CONTEXT BLOAT
Overeating. Huge average contexts → "token bloat" status until habits improve.
FRAGMENTATION
Interrupted naps. Many cold starts → mood penalty.
OVER BUDGET
Foraging limit. Past the soft daily budget the pet is "full" — extra tokens give zero XP.
HEALTHY USAGE
Balanced diet. Full XP, mood up, evolution progress.

Stats — grown from real behavior

VIGOR█████████████░░░░░░░total healthy activity — turns, sessions
FOCUS████████████████░░░░cache-read ratio
WIT█████████░░░░░░░░░░░model-routing quality (right-sized model)
GRIT███████░░░░░░░░░░░░░consecutive active days
SPARK████░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░rare events — new model, new project, night save

+ hidden IVs rolled from DNA at hatch — two pets with identical history still differ.

Progression

Egg hatches after ~3 active days; the dominant playstyle in the hatch window picks the line. Stage 1→2 at level 12, 2→3 at level 30. A dominant stat can branch the final evolution. Neglect never kills — after 7 idle days the pet hibernates and wakes happy. No dead pets after vacation.

Encounters · Trading · Battles — all serverless

ENCOUNTERS — catch by doing
Wild Codelings appear on real events: a new project dir, a new model in the logs, an unusually efficient day. "A wild Glyphit appeared! It joins you if you finish today with >50% cache reuse."
TRADING — .codeling files
aipet trade export writes a versioned JSON file. Share over Slack, gist, a repo. Traded pets earn the "traveler" badge (+XP flavor bonus). Inactive pets live in the Barn.
BATTLES — deterministic, no connection
Each player exports a battle card. Either runs aipet battle <card>. RNG seeds from SHA256(sort(dnaA,dnaB) + UTC-date) — both machines replay the identical fight, turn by turn, in the TUI. Tampered cards that don't re-derive are flagged counterfeit. It's for fun between colleagues, not ranked ladders.

Architecture

cmd/aipet/               CLI: play | daemon | status | dex | trade | battle | config
internal/collector/      KEEP  parses Claude/Codex logs
internal/store/          KEEP  append-only events + stats
internal/pricing/        KEEP  prices "food" (bundled table only)
internal/care/           MORPH was advisor — diet/health rules (pure functions)
internal/sim/            NEW   deterministic pet simulation
  ├─ dna.go              seed, IVs, species roll, Lucent roll
  ├─ tick.go             daily digest → XP/health/mood deltas
  ├─ evolve.go           level & evolution rules
  └─ battle.go           seeded battle resolver
internal/species/        NEW   species table, type chart, movesets (embedded)
internal/save/           NEW   ~/.aipet/pet.json · barn/ · journal.jsonl
internal/daemon/         SLIM  collect loop + sim tick + snapshot (no feed)
internal/tui/            MORPH game screens: Pet | Journal | Dex | Barn
⚖ THE ONE ARCHITECTURAL LAW — DETERMINISM

internal/sim is a pure function of (save state, usage digest, seed). No wall-clock reads inside the sim. No map-iteration-order dependence. No floats in battle math — fixed-point ints. This single rule is what makes serverless battles, replayable saves, and advisor-grade unit testing possible.

Save data

~/.aipet/pet.json (atomic writes) holds DNA, species, stage, level, stats, health, mood, statuses, badges. journal.jsonl is the append-only life log (same pattern as usage.db). The .codeling interchange format is versioned from day one so trades survive upgrades.

  • 2026-07-15The pet moves into Claude Code — host integration shipped. The pet's front door is no longer a separate terminal app: go install … && aipet is now the whole setup, and from there users type /aipet inside Claude Code (or Codex) to see the pet in chat — card injected deterministically via slash-command pre-execution, with Claude adding one line in the pet's voice matched to its mood. The pet also lives in the status line of every Claude Code session (( ^.^ ) Cindling lv4 · cheerful · $0.42 today), and a Stop hook runs a silent incremental collect after every completed turn — collection triggered by the event itself, killing the need for a daemon or a second terminal. Architecture went through an adversarial hardening pass (10 binding review decisions, §8 of HOST_INTEGRATION.md) before a Sonnet agent implemented it: shell-injection defense in depth around $ARGUMENTS (quoting + strict view whitelist + scoped allowed-tools), manifest-driven settings.json merges that never clobber another tool's hooks (backed up, --print dry-run, fully reversible via aipet setup --remove), one freshness owner (30s debounce + skip-never-queue single-flight lock), and the versioning catch that the nested module needs pocs/aipet/v1.1.0-style tags. The agent's concurrency test exposed and fixed a real TOCTOU race in the daemon's pre-existing pidfile lock, and live verification on a real machine caught the ship-blocker the sandbox couldn't: hosts don't inherit a PATH with ~/go/bin, so setup now writes absolute binary paths everywhere (R11). Verified end-to-end in a real Claude Code session: /aipet rendered the user's actual Rivulet (worried, token-bloated, Everfile already in the Dex) and the Stop hook visibly refreshed the snapshot. All gates green: gofmt/vet/-race, 5-target cross-compile, golden-file card tests, 9 setup merge-safety tests.
  • 2026-07-14UX pass + TUI redesign (Fable design pass). Two rounds of hands-on polish driven by live tmux testing of the real binary. Round one fixed the four findings that made the first-run experience fall flat: eggs now hatch from 5 qualifying sessions (an enthusiastic single sitting hatches same-day; a 5-calendar-day safety valve still catches casual users) instead of a flat 3-day wait; the TUI runs its own background collection loop so the pet grows while aipet is open — no second terminal running aipet daemon required, and r now really re-collects; aipet status explains the game on a zero-data machine and prints a one-line pet status; the header face/bubble is driven by the pet's own sim mood, not budget math. Along the way, live testing exposed a pre-existing same-day re-tick no-op bug (once a day had ticked, later cycles that same day were silently ignored — fatal to same-day hatching), fixed with a sealed-baseline replay design: today is always replayed from an end-of-yesterday snapshot, so re-ticking never double-counts streaks or XP. Round two reconciled the community TUI redesign (PR #14) onto that base and applied a Fable design pass: adaptive light/dark color roles, meter/grid/spread/stack layout primitives, responsive two-column layouts ≥90 cols, mood-tinted sprite cards with the face+bubble living inside the card (header face suppressed on the Pet tab — one blinking face at a time), full 30-slot Dex grid even at zero data, consistent first-person empty states, and a per-cycle daemon heartbeat line (16:55:44 +2 events · egg 1/5 sessions) so the foreground daemon never looks hung.
